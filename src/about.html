Our Story

How a garage workshop in Boulder became the outdoor industry's most obsessive engineering lab.

The Beginning

In the spring of 2018, Jamie Chen walked away from a career designing carbon fiber components for satellite launch vehicles. After a decade in aerospace engineering, the pull of the mountains had become impossible to ignore. Working from a rented garage in Boulder, Colorado, Jamie set out to solve a problem that had nagged at every summit attempt and every long-distance trail: outdoor gear that forced you to choose between light weight and lasting durability.

The name came first. Alpenglow is the golden-pink light that illuminates mountain peaks just before sunrise and just after sunset — that brief, luminous window when the world feels both fragile and infinite.

Engineering Philosophy

From day one, the philosophy was clear: ultralight engineering meets lifetime durability. Where other brands cut weight by cutting corners, ${siteTitle} invested in materials science. Jamie's aerospace background meant access to computational modeling tools that most outdoor companies never touch.

"I spent ten years making things that had to survive launch vibrations and thermal cycling in orbit. A backpack only has to survive rain and rock. The engineering principles are the same — you just have to care enough to apply them."

— Jamie Chen, Founder

Tested by Athletes, Not Just Labs

Every ${siteTitle} product must pass the "500-Mile Rule." Before any product reaches production, it accumulates at least 500 miles of real-world trail testing by athletes who have no financial stake in telling the company what it wants to hear.

Our Journey

  1. 2018 — Founded in a Boulder garage. First product: the original Trail Runner Pack 24L.
  2. 2019 — Summit Shelter tent line launches. A PCT thru-hiker’s review goes viral.
  3. 2020 — Opens first factory in Fort Collins, Colorado. 100% renewable energy from day one.
  4. 2021 — Beacon headlamp line launches. Partners with Search & Rescue teams for field testing.
  5. 2022 — Lifetime Repair Promise introduced. Opens repair center in Bozeman, Montana.
  6. 2023 — Reaches 50,000 customers. Launches recycled materials initiative.
  7. 2024 — International expansion to the EU and Japan. Carbon-neutral certification achieved.
  8. 2025 — Opens flagship retail store on Pearl Street in Boulder.
  9. ${year} — Next-generation product lines in development. Over 100 employees.

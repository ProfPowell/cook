You don't need to be a sailor to benefit from knowing a few good knots. These five will cover 95% of the situations you'll encounter on the trail.

## 1. The Bowline

The "king of knots." Creates a fixed loop that won't slip under load but is easy to untie even after bearing heavy weight. Use it for:

- Hanging bear bags
- Tying into an anchor point
- Creating a rescue loop

**How to tie it:** Make a small loop in the standing part. Pass the working end up through the loop, around the standing part, and back down through the loop. Remember: "The rabbit comes out of the hole, goes around the tree, and goes back in the hole."

## 2. The Clove Hitch

Quick, adjustable, and good for temporary attachments. Perfect for:

- Securing a tarp to a trekking pole
- Tying off a clothesline
- Quick camp setup tasks

The clove hitch is fast to tie but can slip under variable loads, so don't rely on it for critical anchors.

## 3. The Taut-Line Hitch

An adjustable knot that slides to tighten or loosen but grips under load. Essential for:

- Tent guy lines
- Adjustable clotheslines
- Any situation where you need tension control

## 4. The Figure-Eight on a Bight

The standard climbing knot. Creates a strong, easy-to-inspect loop. Even if you're not a climber, it's useful for:

- Creating a loop in the middle of a rope
- Anchoring to a fixed point
- Any time you need a loop you can trust your life to

## 5. The Trucker's Hitch

A mechanical advantage system built into a knot. Gives you 3:1 pulling power for:

- Tightening a ridgeline for a tarp
- Securing loads to a vehicle roof rack
- Any time you need something **tight**

---

Practice these at home before you need them on the trail. Tying knots with cold, wet hands in the dark is not the time to learn.

Your outdoor gear is an investment. With proper care, a quality pack, tent, or jacket can last a decade or more. Here's everything you need to know.

## Cleaning Your Pack

Most packs only need a wipe-down with a damp cloth after each trip. For deeper cleaning:

1. Empty all pockets and shake out debris
2. Fill a tub with lukewarm water and mild soap
3. Submerge the pack and gently scrub with a soft brush
4. Rinse thoroughly — soap residue degrades waterproof coatings
5. Hang to air dry, **never** use a dryer

> "The single best thing you can do for your gear is clean it regularly. Dirt and grit act like sandpaper on fabric and zippers." — Riley Nakamura, Head of Product

## Waterproofing

DWR (Durable Water Repellent) coatings wear down over time. Signs it's time to re-treat:

- Water no longer beads on the surface
- Fabric darkens when wet (it's absorbing water)
- Seams feel tacky or show white residue

We recommend a spray-on DWR treatment every 20–30 days of use. Apply to a clean, dry surface and heat-activate with a dryer on low for 20 minutes.

## Storage

**Never** store gear compressed. Stuffing a sleeping bag or tent into its stuff sack for months degrades insulation and waterproof membranes.

- Hang sleeping bags or store them in large cotton sacks
- Store tents loosely rolled in a cool, dry space
- Keep packs upright with all zippers open

## When to Repair vs. Replace

| Issue | Repair | Replace |
|-------|--------|---------|
| Small tear (< 5cm) | Patch with tenacious tape | — |
| Broken zipper slider | Replace slider | — |
| Delaminated waterproofing | — | Replace panel or garment |
| Bent tent pole | Sleeve repair | If cracked at multiple points |
| Frayed straps | Bartack reinforcement | If webbing is cut through |
